About

Ten-plus years operating production infrastructure in regulated and business-critical environments, including medical-device manufacturing. Day-to-day: Linux, virtualisation, Kubernetes, monitoring, and backup/DR with verified restore. Work that has to hold up under change management and audit.

The common thread is assurance: evidence chains and controls mapped to ISO 27001 and NIS2, grounded in hands-on ISMS and BCM work and an ISO 27001 auditor qualification. Given the choice I work open source over closed, mostly on Ubuntu and Red Hat, and stay stack-agnostic wherever the AI integration is viable over an API. The same discipline carries into a self-run homelab, where I explore governed execution, runtime control points, and observability for LLM and agent workloads.

Homelab & Open-Source

Nearly all of this comes out of a self-run, all-open-source fleet of mostly Ubuntu hosts. Local LLM inference on both NVIDIA (llama.cpp and llama-swap) and AMD ROCm (Ollama); a self-built MCP gateway on Hetzner fronting PydanticAI agents, OSINT feeds, and CVE and EU-regulatory intel; a single-node Talos Kubernetes cluster running a VictoriaMetrics, OpenTelemetry, and Wazuh stack for observability and SIEM; ZFS with Sanoid snapshots; a Tailscale mesh.
